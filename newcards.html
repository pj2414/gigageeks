Evidence-Based Practices for Special Education

Auditory Processing Disorder

Auditory Processing Disorder (APD) is a condition where the brain struggles to interpret and process auditory information effectively, leading to difficulties in understanding spoken language. Individuals with APD may face challenges in distinguishing subtle sound differences and often experience issues with following directions or participating in conversations

  • Behavioral interventions, such as token economies and response cost
  • Medication management
  • Cognitive-behavioral therapy
  • Mindfulness-based interventions
  • Parent training and education

It's essential to tailor interventions to individual needs and collaborate with parents, teachers, and other professionals to create a comprehensive support plan.

Dyscalculia

Dyscalculia is a specific learning disorder that impacts an individual's ability to understand and manipulate numbers. Students with dyscalculia may struggle with basic arithmetic operations and mathematical concepts. Evidence-based practices for dyscalculia include:

  • Explicit and systematic instruction in math facts and concepts
  • Multisensory instruction, such as using manipulatives and visual aids
  • Instructional strategies that focus on conceptual understanding, such as number lines and visual models
  • Peer-assisted learning and cooperative learning
  • Computer-assisted instruction

Individualized instruction, ongoing assessment, and targeted interventions are crucial for supporting students with dyscalculia in their mathematical development.

Autism

Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) is a complex neurodevelopmental condition that affects social interaction, communication, and behavior. Evidence-based practices for students with ASD include:

  • Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA)
  • Social Skills Training
  • Visual Supports
  • Structured Teaching
  • Peer-Mediated Instruction and Intervention

Individualized support plans, incorporating these practices, can enhance communication skills, social interactions, and overall well-being for students with autism.
